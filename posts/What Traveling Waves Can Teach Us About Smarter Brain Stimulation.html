What Traveling Waves Can Teach Us About Smarter Brain Stimulation

1 June 2025 by

Ngā Waiata o Te Roro Blog | Neural Interfaces, Performance & Rhythm

A recent study by Haigh et al. (2025) in Cell Reports adds an exciting piece to the puzzle we’ve been working on at Te Kura Rau Mahara: how to make brain stimulation more intelligent — more in sync with the brain’s natural rhythms. Their work with EEG-controlled transcranial magnetic stimulation (TMS) offers strong experimental evidence that neural excitability follows the spatial and temporal contours of traveling waves.

This idea — that the brain’s oscillations don’t just flicker in place but move like waves across the cortex — opens up a rich design space for neuromodulation. Here’s why this matters for our closed-loop EEG-TUS system, and how it might shape the next phase of our work.
